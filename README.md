# Reveal.js Presentation Template  
A simple, modern slide deck you can update using AI

Welcome! This repository contains your presentation template for the end‑of‑semester project.  
You do **not** need to learn Reveal.js, Markdown, HTML, or any coding.  
The system is designed so that you focus on **ideas**, **analysis**, and **explanations** — not formatting.

Your job is to think.  
The AI will help you update the slides.

---

## 🎯 What this template is for

You will use this slide deck to present your work at the end of the semester. Typical content includes:

- graphs or plots you generated  
- explanations of your analysis  
- short videos or animations  
- interactive visualizations  
- references and citations  

The template already includes examples of each. You can replace them with your own content.

---

## 🤖 How to update the slides (AI‑assisted workflow)

You can update this slide deck **without editing the file manually**.

1. Push this repository to your GitHub account.  
2. Open the Colab notebook provided in the course.  
3. Tell the AI what you want to change.  
4. The AI will update `slides.md` for you.  
5. Commit and push the changes.  
6. GitHub Pages will automatically update your presentation.

Examples of things you can tell the AI:

- “Replace the sample graph with my plot from `media/plots/pressure_temp.html`.”  
- “Rewrite Slide 2 to explain the difference between conduction and convection.”  
- “Add a new slide after Slide 3 summarizing my experiment results.”  
- “Update the title slide with my name and email.”  

You can still edit the file manually if you prefer — but you don’t have to.

## 📁 Folder structure

    .
    ├── slides.md          # Your main slide deck (AI will edit this)
    ├── media/
    │   ├── pics/          # Images for your slides
    │   ├── videos/        # Videos or animations
    │   └── plots/         # Interactive HTML plots
    ├── references.bib     # Your bibliography file
    ├── vancouver.csl      # Citation style
    └── index.html         # Used by GitHub Pages to serve the slides

You mainly work with:

- `slides.md` (through AI)  
- files inside the `media/` folder  

---

## 🖼️ What you can replace

**Images**  
Put your images in `media/pics/` and tell the AI which slide to update.

**Videos**  
Place your MP4 files in `media/videos/`.

**Interactive plots**  
Export your plot as HTML and place it in `media/plots/`.

**Text**  
Tell the AI what you want rewritten, expanded, or simplified.

---

## 🎵 About the background music

The title slide includes optional background music.  
It plays only on the first slide and stops when you move forward.  
You may replace the audio file or remove it entirely.

---

## 📚 Citations

If your project uses references, add them to `references.bib`.  
The bibliography on the last slide will update automatically.

---

## 🧪 Previewing your slides

Once you push your changes, GitHub Pages will automatically build your presentation.  
You can view it at:

    https://<your-username>.github.io/<your-repo-name>/

This link updates every time you push.

- Keep slides clean and focused.  
- Use visuals to support your explanation.  
- Let the AI help with wording and structure.  
- Focus on insights, not decoration.  
- Practice your flow — the slides will take care of themselves.
